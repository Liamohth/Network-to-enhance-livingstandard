Get to know what our mission is!

our was mission

We are striving to serve humanity by alleviating illiteracy, poverty, and social injustice. We are trying to enable society to take responsibility for the situation that deprives its citizens of reaching their full potential. The society that is to motivate and seek resolutions through collective actions is by engaging the people in helping them to realize their potential.

our was vision

We envision a world with adequate food, minimum illiteracy and protected social justice. A healthy society is a society that create environment to protect its citizens. A society that protect, build an respect, dignity, justice and equality for all.

our was VALUES

We believe in rapid actions to end poverty and illiteracy worldwide. Citizens rights to engage In the establishment of the society, whether it be the economy, education and agricultural we valued the citizens of reaching their full potential.

Learn more about network to enhance live standard(NELS)

Network to Enhance Living Standard (NELS) is a registered NGO based in the United States of America (USA) with an office in Liberia where day to day activities are run. Founded in 2017, NELS has been very influential and dedicated to working on issues ranging from Water, Sanitation and Hygiene, Agriculture and Education to Rehabilitation /Construction, peace building and Human Rights. With a Team of Dedicated and skilled Professionals we have been able to achieve much in few years. NELS has reached out to over 12,000 disadvantaged children and over 7,000 adults directly.


NELS is currently working in Liberia with
tentacle grip on Education and WASH activities.

AS part of our educational programs, NELS is distributing educational materials including, copy books, pens and pencils, book bags and eraser. All of these are made possible through the voluntary contribution of our Members and dedicated Board of Directors and most especially our CEO.

Moreover, NELS is finalizing negotiations with top business investor Tonka Trading Company for the Rehabilitation and elevation of the Kwendi Vocational Training Center (KVTC) into a Technical College which will be the first Technical College offering Bachelor degree in courses like Mechanized Farming and etc.

Our community development program has assist in improving Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH) in Stockton Creek and Bong Mines Bridge communities respectively by cleaning the main drainage system that connects the two communities. Prior to NELS intervention, flooding was the only alternative for these residents.

With a focused approach to dealing with the complex multifaceted reality and the ambiguous terrain of poverty, illiteracy and social injustice, we are resolved to working with NGOs, CBOs and Investors in alleviating these problems.
